My Journey Through the Stars

This short text chronicles the past few years of my life, detailing what I’ve done, where I’ve worked, and how I’ve enjoyed my time. It's a bit rough around the edges and modestly honest, but if you're interested and have the time, this is my story.

2024

In March 2024, I completed my final coding project, ft_transcendence, from the main curriculum. My next project? Applying for jobs—a task I'm still tackling. The big question looming over me: "What do I want to do?". Fast forward three months, and here I am, writing this little, perhaps inconsequential, text about myself while simultaneously launching my first portfolio website for the world to see. Crazy, right? It started as a fun endeavor but soon became an all-consuming project with countless details to perfect. In the frenzy, I completely neglected the act of applying for internships.


Aside from that, I revisited some old passions. I began playing the piano again and resumed learning Spanish, both with modest success. I also participated in my first chess tournament out of sheer curiosity. I'm neither terrible nor excellent—I just didn't prepare adequately. Maybe my lack of routine is hampering my productivity and discipline, or perhaps it's the allure of the YouTube algorithm. One day, I believe I’ll have it all figured out (winking smiley).

2023

This was a very interesting and eventful year.

It began unlike any other. With new responsibilities to manage, numerous changes, and some mild stress related to my time at 42. While it might not sound fun to some, I take life at a relaxed pace and try not to stress too much. If I had to describe the year in one word, it would be: amazeballs. Changes are a natural part of life, stress is manageable, and living in the moment truly helped.

From moving out of my parents' house, playing tunes at parties, and enjoying video games with friends, to visiting various places, participating in hackathons, and tackling the challenges of Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions—2023 was filled with memorable experiences. These events taught me a lot and helped me grow in countless ways.
